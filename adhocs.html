Dinner and Dance 2020

Date to be confirmed

The annual Dinner and Dance is an important event as it marks the start of a new and exciting journey for the freshmen. Normally held on the weekend of Week 4/5 of Semester 1, it is one of the biggest events in hall with close to 200 attendees! The D&D is accompanied with our Hall Pageant where pageantees from the various bloods strut the stage and put on performances to show off their unique personalities. At the end of the night, our new Hall King and Queen will be crowned. Of course, the night doesn't end there as all event attendees are invited to the much anticipated after-party! It is definitely a night to remember (or not)! The event serves at a platform whereby hall residents, both old and new, can forge new friendships and create new memories, instilling a greater sense of belonging to our home away from home. The D&D committee welcomes you to our Hall 12 family!

HOPE 2020

Date to be confirmed

HOPE is a committee that seeks to foster the spirit of volunteerism in our residents and to give back to our society as much as we can! Our past projects include organising a Christmas party for the elderly at MINDS in 2018 and volunteering at the Willing Hearts Soup Kitchen to assist with the preparation and cooking of meals for the less fortunate beneficiaries in 2019. Last year, we wanted to try something different with the inaugural Waste-Less Week Challenge which was a huge success! The challenge was created to encourage our Residents to be more conscious of the waste we're producing on a daily basis and promote less wasteful lifestyles. The 7 day Instagram challenge was met with overwhelming responses with more than 133k impressions being made throughout our campaign. Have any interesting ideas or community project you would like to embark on? Join us. Let's Be Good, Do Good, Feel Good!

Overseas Voluntary Expedition (OVE) 2021

Date to be confirmed

Hall 12 Overseas Voluntary Expedition (OVE) is an annual local and overseas CIP project that takes place throughout the entire year! Our teams of 20-25 Twelvers will plan, crowdfund and execute several local CIP projects, such as volunteering at Assisted Living Facilities or holding donation drives! Our previous project (2019) was to Dagupan, Philippines. Our project was centered around a village school, where we helped set up an aquaponics system to facilitate the growth of crops which the village could sell. We also conducted different lessons for the students and also had homestays, where we stayed with the families in the village and spent 2 days in their shoes! OVE is definitely one of the BEST experiences I've had in Hall 12 and NTU! Looking forward to 2020, our project revolves around building a uphill path and installing solar panels in a village along the Mekong River in Laos!
